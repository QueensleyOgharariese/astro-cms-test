There are games you finish and immediately start again. Collar x Malice is one of them. Set in a near-future Tokyo under lockdown after a series of brutal crimes, it follows police officer Ichika Hoshino as she gets drawn into an investigation involving five complex, flawed, completely unforgettable men.

## Why this game is different

Collar x Malice doesn't pull its punches. The themes are heavy — violence, corruption, justice vs revenge — and the writers don't shy away from putting the heroine in real danger. Ichika is also genuinely competent, which makes a refreshing change.

## The route that changed everything

I won't name names (no spoilers), but one of the five routes sent me into a spiral I wasn't prepared for. It's the kind of storytelling that reminds you why you fell in love with visual novels in the first place.

## Should you play it?

Yes. A thousand times yes. Just have snacks and tissues ready.
